Introduce

"firmitas, utilitas, venustas."

 The ancient Roman architect Vitruvius once identified the three elements of architecture as stated above. Doesn't it seem similar to development?
 Before I fell in love with development, I was fond of architecture. Architecture may be seen merely as building structures, but in fact, a single piece of architecture implies an intention to create "a space sufficiently independent from the external environment where people can feel comfortable and perform activities suitable for the purpose of that space". In this regard, how this space was designed with what intent or meaning, how it is intended to appear, and how actual people are supposed to use it are all considered during construction until it becomes a final architectural structure. But I think this is similar to development. If we take a developed application as an example, it's "a space created for actions suitable for user's purpose", and while implementing this, developers consider what actions users will take and how they want it to appear. Ultimately, both architecture and development create optimized living environments for humans through the fusion of various technologies. As a child who was charmed by architecture at one point dreamed of becoming an architect. However, without deeply considering why I liked it so much; I gave up due to adults' opinions that being an architect in Korea is challenging. Why do I find development fun now? It might be because it's similar to architecture. In both fields -architecture and development- I enjoy understanding what someone has implemented or developed intended to look like or mean; making them my own through this process also leaves great value for me as an action leaving tremendous accomplishment. Furthermore, If someone uses what I have implemented or developed and understands its intentionality while contemplating its appearance; that would be overwhelmingly fulfilling.
 Along with my passion for development considering stability, usefulness beauty or beyond those elements; by continuously investing effort into writing good code - I dream about becoming someone whose value increases over time creating an amazing learning curve.
